Use Cases

The SDK is live across two workloads.

Account takeover, business email compromise, synthetic identity, payment fraud — all originate somewhere else, by definition. Sanctions screening, securities offerings, content licensing, gaming compacts — all turn on where the user actually is. Octet replaces guesswork with a hardware-signed predicate against the policy you set.

01· Workload

Jurisdictional compliance and geofencing.

A sanctions screen runs on an IP address and a country code from a dropdown. A five-dollar VPN defeats it. Major IP geolocation databases disagree on the same address by an average of 620 kilometres. The largest sanctions fine in banking history was $8.9 billion. A recent one was $4.3 billion, and the CEO did prison time. OFAC violations are strict liability. Octet replaces the IP heuristic with a hardware-signed predicate over presence.

01.01

National exclusion: blocking the United States

Unregistered securities offerings, certain derivatives, unlicensed gambling, and tokens that meet the Howey test all carry federal exposure. SEC Regulation S requires the seller to hold a reasonable belief that the buyer is outside the US. IP geolocation does not meet a defensible reasonable-belief standard. Self-attestation does not either.

Octet issues a hardware-signed proof before any session opens. The proof covers Puerto Rico, Guam, American Samoa, the US Virgin Islands, and the Northern Mariana Islands. Verifiable offline. Retained for audit. No raw coordinates leave the device.

Predicateoutside(us_polygon)
RegimeReg S · Howey · ATS · broker-dealer
01.02

Sanctioned jurisdiction blocking

OFAC and EU restrictive measures impose strict liability. Seventy-one million people in sanctioned jurisdictions live within 100 km of a border with a non-sanctioned territory. IP databases disagree on the same address by an average of 620 km — wider than the border between nearly every sanctioned and unsanctioned country on earth.

A regulator asking for evidence gets a signed predicate, not an IP heuristic.

RegimeOFAC · EU restrictive measures
01.03

State-by-state activation

A single platform can serve only the jurisdictions where it holds authorisation, or where federal preemption has been judicially confirmed. The geofence updates without a client release as state injunctions issue or lift. The same primitive supports county-level and municipal carve-outs, and tribal land where gaming compacts apply.

01.04

Sub-political zones: prisons, schools, stadiums

Octet supports polygon definitions at any scale, from a single building to a campus to a stadium bowl. The same cryptographic guarantee as a national geofence. Operators publish their polygons; any compliant app honours them.

02· Workload

Fraud prevention.

Account takeover, business email compromise, synthetic identities, AI-driven impersonation, payment fraud — every one originates somewhere else, by definition. When a proof of presence is bound to the device, the attacker fails. Generative AI has industrialised fraud; the only shield against perfect copies is ground truth that cannot be reproduced.

02.01

Account takeover (invisible login)

Octet runs alongside your existing auth. When the device produces a valid presence proof, your app can skip the push notification, the SMS code, the step-up challenge.

A criminal in Lagos logs in with stolen credentials and spoofs a Chicago IP to match the account holder. The IP checks pass. The device cannot produce a valid presence proof for Chicago. The proof fails. Your stack catches it. The real customer never sees a challenge.
